# TrainTimer Logo Exploration 05

## Design role

This board is a brand-asset comparison, not a webpage redesign. Its only job is to compare three app-icon treatments that retain the selected U/M cube T geometry.

## Position questions

- Narrative role: primary identity mark for a professional speedcubing training launcher.
- Viewing distance: mostly 16–76 px in the macOS menu, Finder, Dock, launcher header, and Spotlight; the 1024 px view is for craft review only.
- Visual temperature: precise, controlled, technical, and mature. The Liquid Glass option may feel more luminous, but not playful or decorative.
- Capacity: one dominant T, one complete 3×3 cube reference, and at most one blue registration signal. No extra numerals, initials, arrows, speed lines, or secondary symbols.

## Shared geometry

- Canvas: 1024×1024.
- Cubie: 176×176 with 32 px radius.
- Grid gap: 20 px.
- Active U layer: all three cells in the top row.
- Active M slice: middle cells in rows two and three.
- Inactive cube reference: the four remaining corner cells.
- Optical center: 512×512.
- Signature signal: one short blue registration line between U and M.

## Variants

1. **Precision Line** — a light titanium plate, low-contrast 3×3 wireframe, and a heavier outlined T. It should feel like an instrument diagram or calibration overlay.
2. **Competition Flat** — a dark, fully flat icon with no gradients, highlights, blur, or shadow. It prioritizes 16–32 px recognition and practical reproduction.
3. **Liquid Layer** — an Apple-aligned layered treatment. Import sources stay flat and opaque; the comparison board supplies a static material preview only. The native version should ultimately be authored in Icon Composer with background, inactive cube, U, M, and registration assets separated by depth.

## Constraints

- Preserve the already approved U/M cube T concept.
- Keep every cubie on the shared grid.
- Use only the established TrainTimer palette: slate, cool white, and registration blue.
- Do not replace the current production App icon until the user selects a variant.
- Do not modify any `public/` webpage logo or favicon.
